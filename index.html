Peru Tips

Traveling to Peru

Aeropuerto Internacional Jorge Chávez (LIM) is the main airport hub for international flights to Peru. Travel from the airport to the city center can be done by car only. We recommend taking one of the taxis from the taxi companies in the arrivalterminal (right after customs) or arranging a pickup with your hotel prior to your arrival. Expect around one hour of travel time by car from the airport to Miraflores or Barranco.

Traveling in Peru

With incredible biodiverse regions to choose from, the possibilities for travel in Peru are virtually endless. It’s up to you if you want to visit the coastal desert, discover the Andes, or the Amazon jungle (or all three!). Below is our list of suggested tourist destinations.

  • Mountains

    Peru is home to the largest portion of the Andes. Home to Cuzco, Machu Picchu, the Sacred Valley, Arequipa, and Titicaca, an Andean adventure is a must if you come to Peru for the first time.

    • Cuzco, Machu Picchu, & Sacred Valley of the Inkas

      To discover the impressive architectural feats of the Inkas, and learn about this Andean culture’s way of life immersed in breath-taking mountain views is no less than life-changing. Be very respectful of the altitude, and make sure you take it super easy the first day (by super easy I mean rest all day and drink coca leaf tea). Your body needs time to adjust to the altitude. If you already know you’re sensitive to high altitudes, fly to Cusco from Lima, and descend immediately to the sacred valley. You can take the train to Machu Picchu from there, and then do the city of Cuzco on the last day. If you’re seriously adventurous, you can plan very ahead of time and do the proper Inka trail. You can choose to do the 3 day, 5 day, or full week hike, where you’ll climb up the Machu Picchu mountain and arrive at the Sun Gate of Machu Picchu at sunrise. Just make sure you book ahead of time and be honest about your fitness level. Climbing at high altitude is no easy feat.

    • Puno & Lake Titicaca

      The region of Puno is home to the highest navigable body of water in the world. Lake Titicaca is considered one of the most beautiful places on earth, no wonder the Inkas believed it was the birthplace of their main deity, the sun. The people who inhabit the lake do so on islands made of reeds since Precolombian times. Again, be respectful of the altitude, and take it easy your first day. You can travel from Cuzco to Tititcaca by luxury train. Alternatively, you can also take a bus. Expect about 1 day of travel.

    • Arequipa

      Called the white city, this beautiful colonial city in the mountains is home to the Misti Volcano and the famous Colca Canyon, home of the Peruvian Condor. Explore old Peruvian monasteries, eat amazing food at their picanterías, hike the Cordillera Blanca, and enjoy the Colca from thermal baths under the stars.

  • Jungle

    • Tambopata Reserve & Manu National Park

      Tambopata is a national reserve in the south of the Amazon basin. You can enjoy the incredible flora and fauna of the Amazon jungle in one of the many eco-lodges on the shores of the Madre de Dios River. Adjacent to the Tambopata reserve is the Manu National Reserve, known for its rich biodiversity. Home to jaguars, macaws, black caimans, and anacondas, it’s a must if you’re interested in jungle fauna.

    • Iquitos

      Iquitos is the largest city in the world that cannot be reached by road. Called the gateway to the jungle, you can fly here to experience a vibrant capital before embarking on an Amazon journey.

    • Kuelap & Gocta

      In the southern part of the Amazonas region, you can visit the Chachapoyas fortress Kuelap and visit the incredible waterfalls of Gocta. Some say the beauty of Kuelap rivals that of Machu Picchu.

  • Coast

    The coastal desert of Peru is home to the legacies of the Pre-Columbian cultures of Paracas and Nazca. It is also home to beautiful and peaceful beaches in the north like Máncora.

    • Paracas, Nazca & Huacachina

      The bay of Paracas is home to a natural reserve with incredible beaches, and the opportunity to do boat tours of the Ballestas Islands, home to Humboldt penguins, sea lions and the mysterious candelabra in the sand. Huacachina is a desert oasis surrounded by beautiful sand dunes, one hour south of Paracas. It’s unbelievably fun to climb in one of the sand buggies and drive through the beautiful landscape of sand dunes. Surrounded by desert, one feels more in a Star Wars movie than in Peru. Nazca is home to the mystical Nazca lines. To this day, no one knows how the Nazca people managed to carve these kilometric lines in the sand, which remain intact to this day. To see them in full, you have to board an aeroplane and look at them from the air. Common folklore says that the Nazca people were aided by aliens in their construction of the lines, and once you see them, you’ll be tempted to share that belief.

    • Mancora

      Mancora is a beautiful beach in the north of Peru. A haven for surfers and beach bums, it’s a great place to eat fresh fish, hang out, and and swim.

  • Lima

    • Sleep

      We strongly recommend booking a hotel in Miraflores or Barranco. Adjacent to each other overlooking the sea, together they make up the cultural hub of Lima. Most wedding festivity events will take place in Miraflores. In Barranco you can enjoy beautiful walks on the Malecón overlooking the ocean. The scene at night in Barranco is super lively, home to cozy bars and amazing restaurants.

      • The Marriott Courtyard

    • Eat

      Perhaps the most important thing you need to do in Lima is experience the city’s amazing food. Peruvian cuisine is spectacular, and Lima boasts some of the world’s best restaurants. From traditional Peruvian to Nikkei, these restaurants represent what we most miss living abroad.

      • La Trattoria di Mambrino

        Clearly biased here. Ugo and Sandra Plevisani started La Trattoria as a tiny restaurant nearly 30 years ago. Now moved to Larcomar, it remains a staple of incredible Italian cuisine in Lima. website

      • Mercado

        Ceviche is meant to be eaten during daytime, so cevicherias are closed at night. Queue up here for chef Rafael Osterling’s variations of ceviche, tiradito, and grilled seafood for lunch. Worth the wait. website

      • Maido

        The best place to try Nikkei food, the incredible marriage of Peruvian and Japanese cuisines. Both traditions are an ode to fresh fish, and once you eat here you’ll understand why. website

      • Osaka

        Upscale Japanese restaurant serving Nikkei food. The hip spot to eat and be seen. website

      • Paseo Colón

        Great place to enjoy Latin-American and Peruvian fusion dishes. The terrace is a lively spot to get a great Pisco Sour and enjoy their incredible corn-pie. website

      • Canta Rana

        An institution in Barranco known for their ceviche. The wood-paneling is full of soccer paraphernalia, and may be the coziest spot to get lunch in Barranco. Get the ceviche mixto, the tiradito, and chicharrón de calamar. website

      • Rafael

        Upscale Peruvian-French fusion cuisine. This is chef Rafael Osterling’s signature restaurant, and a sure-shot if you’re feeling fancy. website

      • Isolina Taberna Peruana

        Restaurant in Barranco famed for serving old-school hearty Peruvian classics. Definitely not light food, but totally worth it. If the line is long, we suggest you wait. website

      • Jerónimo

        Incredible fusion of Peruvian and Mexican cuisines. website

      • La Picantería

        Great restaurant if you love fish. You pick one from the cooler and they make three dishes of your choice out of it. Their fried fish ‘chicharrón’ is among the best. website

      • Restaurante Fiesta

        Incredible restaurant to enjoy delicacies of Chiclayo by chef Hector Solís. Famous for their ‘grilled ceviche’! A must. website

      • Chifa Titi

        Chinese-Peruvian restaurants, called ‘Chifas’, are endemic to Perú and ubiquitous around the country. You have to know where to go. Chifa Titi is the absolute best! Their Peking duck is out of this world. website

      • La Bodeguita de la Trattoria

        Great cafe for salads, pastas, and sandwiches. Their outpost in front of la Huaca Pucllana is our absolute favorite because of the beautiful terrace. Don’t forget to get dessert! website

      • Costanera700

        Credited with founding Nikkei cuisine, this spot remains a local favorite. Call in advance if you’d like their famous fish in a salt crust. website

      • Chez Wong

        This hole in the wall is an institution. Chef Javier Wong filets the fish in front of you before he cuts it up into a ceviche or a dish of the day. Some may say its the best ceviche in the world. website

      • Lucio

        Coffee shop with Sandra Plevisani’s amazing desserts. Stop by for breakfast (the french toast is to die for) or for afternoon tea and cake. Our favorites are the dark chocolate mini cheesecakes. website

      • Amaz

        Restaurant devoted to Amazonian dishes. Try the chonta salad. website

      • Edo

        Everyday sushi place. Great for a quick and cheap lunch. website

      • Pan, Sal, Aire

        Low key spot for lunch and dinner. They have great sandwiches for lunch, and pizza for dinner. Great to come with friends for a chill meal and a glass of wine in a hip setting. website

      • Pan de la Chola

        Great place for breakfast and lunch if you like a good sandwich. They make the best croissant in town. website

    • Culture

      There’s a lot to do culturally in Lima beyond eating amazing food.

      • The Historic Center

        Visit the Cathedral and its catacombs, the Plaza de San Martín, the MALI (national art museum), and Chinatown.

      • Museo Larco

        Incredible museum of Pre Columbian art. website

      • Museo Amano

        Amazing museum of Pre Columbian textiles. website

      • MAC

        Museum of contemporary art in Barranco. website

    • Shops

      Here are our top suggestions for shops

      • Las Pallas Artesanía

        Mari Solari is a Scottish lady who devoted her life to sourcing the most beautiful high-end Peruvian handicrafts. This shop in an old colonial house is a must. location

      • Escudo Perú

        Founded by the Macchiavello sisters Giuliana and Chiara, Escudo is a fashion brand which uses traditional weaving techniques to create hand woven unique pieces. Visit their showroom in Barranco to buy items from their collection. website

      • Mercado Indio

        The Indian market is, the one stop shop for Peruvian handicrafts. The offering is impressive! Prepare to stay at least an hour navigating the hundreds of vendor booths. location

      • Ilaria

        Peru is the second largest producer of silver in the world. Backed by this tradition, artist Ilaria Ciabatti makes incredible objects out of silver. They have a shop in Larcomar. website

      • Kuna

        If you want to buy premium alpaca knits, you need to go to Kuna. They use the only the best fabrics to create beautiful pieces. You can find their shop in Larcomar. website

      • Larcomar

        Huge shopping mall overlooking the ocean. You’ll be able to find Ilaria, Kuna, and other Peruvian shops here as well as classics from abroad. website
